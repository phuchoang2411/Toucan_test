# Field Visits Now Work the Way Field Work Actually Happens

*(Vietnamese version: [`Executive Brief.vi.md`](./Executive%20Brief.vi.md))*

## The problem

**A rep's story.** Linh visits a bakery, but the owner isn't there — or asks her to come back next week instead. Before this update, Linh had no way to touch that one visit. The only way to change anything was to reopen the bakery's entire record and uncheck "schedule a visit" — which didn't just cancel today's visit, it wiped out *every other visit already planned for that bakery*. So in practice, reps just left broken visits sitting on the schedule and moved on. The schedule stopped reflecting reality.

**A manager's story.** A manager opens the dashboard and sees "Linh has 3 overdue visits." That's the end of the story — there's no way to click through and see which three, or do anything about them. The dashboard could show a problem but never let a manager act on it.

## What changed

**Reps can now manage one visit at a time.** From any planned visit, a rep can reschedule it to a new date or cancel it — without touching anything else on that outlet's schedule. Cancelling asks for a reason: customer postponed, no-show, planned by mistake, or other. That reason is saved and shown wherever the visit appears, so a cancelled visit tells its own story instead of just disappearing.

**Every number on the manager dashboard is now clickable.** See "Linh has 3 overdue visits"? Click it — you land on exactly those three visits, ready to review. Click a sales-funnel stage on the dashboard, and you see exactly the outlets sitting in that stage. The dashboard went from a snapshot to a starting point.

## Why it matters

- **The schedule becomes trustworthy again.** Reps no longer have to work around the app — they use it the way the job actually works, so what's on the schedule reflects what's really happening in the field.
- **No-shows and postponements become visible data**, not silent losses. Over time, this can surface patterns — like which outlets or areas keep no-showing — that were invisible before.
- **Managers move from noticing a problem to fixing it**, in one click instead of a manual search.
- **Nothing about the audit trail changes.** Every reschedule or cancellation still keeps the full history and evidence tied to that visit — nothing is lost, only made easier to act on.

## See it in 2 minutes

1. Open a planned visit and click **Reschedule** — pick a new date, confirm, and the schedule updates instantly.
2. Open a different planned visit, click **Cancel visit**, choose **No-show** as the reason, and confirm — the visit flips to "cancelled," and the reason is visible right there.
3. Go to the dashboard, click a rep's overdue count, and land on exactly those overdue visits.
4. Click a funnel stage bar, and land on exactly the outlets in that stage.

## Status

Delivered and covered by automated tests. Existing workflows (creating outlets, scheduling first visits, recording evidence) are unchanged — this only adds new ways to act on what's already there.
